== Purpose

This repository documents a complete Neural DSP Quad Cortex setup for a modern metalcore/deathcore band with two 7-string guitars and bass. The target is a dry, tight, aggressive band sound where palm mutes hit hard, powerchords stay defined, and the bass supplies the true low-end mass without making the guitars muddy.

Each instrument folder uses `README.adoc` as its index. The guitar folders keep one separate file per scene. The bass is intentionally a single large README because the split low/high routing is easier to understand when viewed as one continuous signal chain.

== Design principles

[%autowidth,cols="1,2,3",options="header"]
|===
|Principle |Implementation |Reason
|Guitars stay dry in rhythm scenes |No delay, chorus, reverb, doubler, or stereo widening on rhythm. |Modern heavy rhythm tones need immediate attack and repeatable transient shape. Time effects make palm mutes feel wider but less tight.
|Low-end weight comes from bass |Guitar low cuts live around 80 to 95 Hz depending on scene. Bass clean-low path carries 35 to 90 Hz. |This prevents two guitars and bass from stacking uncontrolled low mids. The band sounds larger because each instrument owns a defined range.
|Two guitars are not identical |6505 side uses a tighter TRAD 421 + 57 cab blend. EV101 side uses a bigger OS-V2 57 + 121 cab blend. |Two identical high-gain tones can blur together. Complementary voicings produce a wider, clearer stereo image.
|Hot active pickups are controlled at the input |Start guitars at -6.0 dB input gain and keep amp gain moderate. |Fishman Fluence Modern pickups can overload early gain stages. Lower gain improves note separation and keeps palm mutes from turning into low-mid mush.
|Scene switching happens at the Mixer |Inactive paths are muted with Mixer Level A/B rather than relying only on bypass states. |This prevents clean DI leakage, amp path leakage, and inconsistent tails when switching between rhythm, clean, and lead.
|Final Gain handles scene volume |Each scene has final Gain values for level matching. |Amp gain and compressor makeup change tone and feel. Final Gain changes level with minimal tone change.
|===


== Quick build order

. Import the recommended third-party IRs into the Quad Cortex IR Library.
. Build the two guitar presets first and confirm rhythm scenes work before adding clean and lead effects.
. Build the bass split preset and check the clean-low path alone before bringing in the dirty path.
. Match rhythm levels between both guitars with final Gain blocks.
. Bring in bass and adjust the bass 240 Hz area before changing the guitar presets.
. Set lead scene boosts at rehearsal volume. Bedroom levels often make lead scenes seem louder than they are in a full band.

== How to judge the presets

Do not judge the guitar rhythm presets alone with bass muted. The guitar tones are intentionally leaner than a finished record when soloed. In context, the bass supplies the sub-low weight and the guitars supply pick attack, upper-mid aggression, and tight chord information.

Use this check:

[%autowidth,cols="1,2,3",options="header"]
|===
|Check |Expected result |Fix if wrong
|Guitars alone |Tight, slightly lean, clear palm mutes. |If they sound huge alone but cloudy with bass, reduce guitar 220 to 360 Hz.
|Bass alone |Low path is stable, dirty path is grindy and somewhat thin. |If the dirty path sounds full alone, it probably has too much low end.
|Full band |Palm mutes get heavier when bass enters, not muddier. |If guitars shrink when bass enters, cut bass 150 to 300 Hz before boosting guitars.
|Lead scenes |Lead sits above rhythm without washing out fast passages. |Reduce delay mix and reverb mix before reducing lead gain.
|===
