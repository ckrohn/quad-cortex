== Target

The 6505 lead scene keeps the aggressive PV-505 character but adds mid push, less gating, controlled delay, and a small volume lift. The lead scene uses Path A only. It keeps the rhythm cab identity but changes gain, EQ, gate, delay, reverb, and final level.

== Active routing

[source,text]
----
Path A active:
Green 808 -> PV-505 Lead -> Adaptive Gate (S/C) -> IR Loader: Single (M)

Path B muted at Mixer.

Post Mixer:
Low-High Cut -> Parametric-8 -> Digital Delay (M) -> Plate -> Gain -> Output

Chief CE2W (M) is bypassed by default in the lead scene.
----

== Green 808

[%autowidth,cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
|Parameter |Setting |Why this value is used
|Overdrive |0.15 |A small increase gives sustain and touch sensitivity without making the pedal a fuzz source.
|Tone |5.0 |Slightly darker than rhythm to avoid scratchy high notes.
|Level |7.2 |Still pushes the amp, but a little less than rhythm so the extra amp gain does not over-compress.
|Bypass |Off |The boost remains part of the lead feel.
|===


The lead boost settings are close to rhythm but slightly altered for sustain and smoothness. The overdrive value remains very low because too much pedal drive makes fast leads smaller and more compressed.

== PV-505 Lead

[%autowidth,cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
|Parameter |Setting |Why this value is used
|Gain |3.8 |Adds sustain over rhythm while staying below the mushy range for fast technical leads.
|Bass |2.0 |Lower than rhythm so the solo does not add low-end clutter over rhythm guitar and bass.
|Mid |6.4 |Lead focus comes mainly from midrange, not from extra volume alone.
|Treble |4.9 |Slightly more bite than rhythm for note edge.
|Master |3.2 |A small increase adds body and sustain.
|Resonance |1.5 |Lower than rhythm because resonance makes leads wider but less focused.
|Presence |3.7 |Slightly reduced to smooth the upper end with delay and reverb.
|Level |Unity, then trim with final Gain |Keep block level stable and use final Gain for the scene boost.
|===


The lead scene gets more mids and slightly more gain, but less low-end resonance than rhythm. This places the lead above the band without adding low-frequency clutter.

== Adaptive Gate (S/C)

[%autowidth,cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
|Parameter |Setting |Why this value is used
|Noise Reduction |76.0% |Lower than rhythm so bends, vibrato, legato, and harmonics sustain naturally.
|Source / Trigger |In 1 |The gate follows the clean DI transient.
|Bypass |Off |Still needed for high-gain noise, but less aggressive than rhythm.
|===


If sustain is still being cut off, lower Noise Reduction to `70%` to `72%`. Do this before adding more amp gain.

== Post-mixer Low-High Cut

[%autowidth,cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
|Parameter |Setting |Why this value is used
|Low Cut |95 Hz |Keeps the solo out of the bass and rhythm-guitar low-end space.
|High Cut |7.8 kHz |Darker than clean, tight enough to keep delay repeats smooth.
|===


The lead low cut is higher than rhythm because the lead should sit on top of the rhythm section, not add another low-mid guitar layer.

Lead EQ emphasizes the vocal midrange and reduces low-mid congestion. The purpose is audibility, not simply more gain.

== Digital Delay (M)

[%autowidth,cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
|Parameter |Setting |Why this value is used
|Mix |14% |Enough wet signal for sustain, but still dry enough for fast runs.
|Feedback |30% |Creates several repeats without becoming ambient.
|High Pass |240 Hz |Keeps low notes and bends from building up in the delay path.
|Low Pass |4200 Hz |Keeps repeats behind the dry lead.
|Sync |Off |Fixed time keeps the lead from sounding overly grid-locked.
|Sync Note |Ignored when Sync is Off |No tempo subdivision is active.
|Delay Time |430 ms |A lead-length delay that sits apart from the EV101 side.
|Mod Rate |0.20 Hz |Very slow movement, mainly to soften the repeats.
|Mod Depth |8% |Low modulation so fast lead lines do not sound pitch-wobbly.
|Trails |On |Lets repeats decay naturally when leaving the lead scene.
|Bypass |Off |Delay is active for lead.
|===


Lead delay adds sustain and dimension. The repeats are high-passed and low-passed so they remain behind the dry lead and do not add fizz or low-mid mud.

== Plate

[%autowidth,cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
|Parameter |Setting |Why this value is used
|Mix |8% |Low reverb amount. Delay provides most of the space.
|Decay |48% |Shorter than clean so fast leads stay defined.
|Pre Delay |20.0 ms |Separates the dry lead from the reverb tail.
|High Pass |250 Hz |Keeps low-end reverb out of the rhythm section.
|Low Pass |3600 Hz |Darkens the tail enough to avoid fizz.
|LF Damping |55% |Controls low-frequency tail length.
|HF Damping |70% |Stronger high damping keeps the lead reverb smooth.
|Mod Depth |8% |Minimal movement for a stable lead tone.
|Trails |On |Natural decay when switching back to rhythm.
|Bypass |Off |Reverb is active but intentionally low.
|===


Lead reverb is intentionally much lower than clean reverb. In this style, delay supplies most of the space, while a small plate prevents the lead from sounding pasted on top of the mix.

== Lead correction workflow

[%autowidth,cols="2,2,3",options="header"]
|===
|Problem |Change |Reason
|Lead sustain gets chopped |Lower Adaptive Gate to 70 to 72% |The gate is more likely than the amp to kill sustain.
|Lead is buried |Raise final Gain by +0.5 to +1.0 dB or add +1 dB in the primary lead-mid band |Volume and mids solve audibility better than extra gain.
|Lead is fizzy |Lower High Cut to 7.4 to 7.6 kHz or cut the 3.7 to 3.9 kHz band another -1 dB |This controls harshness while preserving note focus.
|Delay clouds fast runs |Lower Delay Mix to 8 to 10% and Feedback to 20 to 24% |Fewer repeats create more clarity.
|Lead feels too small |Raise mids before raising bass or resonance |Mids increase perceived size without fighting bass guitar.
|===
