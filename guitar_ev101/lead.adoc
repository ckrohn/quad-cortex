== Target

The EV101 lead scene is the smoother lead voice. It gets mid focus and delay, but less wetness than the 6505 lead so harmonized lines stay separated. The lead scene uses Path A only. It keeps the rhythm cab identity but changes gain, EQ, gate, delay, reverb, and final level.

== Active routing

[source,text]
----
Path A active:
Green 808 -> EV101IIIS Red 6L6 100W -> Adaptive Gate (S/C) -> IR Loader: Single (M)

Path B muted at Mixer.

Post Mixer:
Low-High Cut -> Parametric-8 -> Digital Delay (M) -> Plate -> Gain -> Output

Chief CE2W (M) is bypassed by default in the lead scene.
----

== Green 808

[%autowidth,cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
|Parameter |Setting |Why this value is used
|Overdrive |0.10 |Small sustain increase without making the boosted EV101 too compressed.
|Tone |5.4 |Darker than rhythm to keep high notes smooth.
|Level |7.3 |Slightly lower than rhythm because the amp gain is higher.
|Bypass |Off |The boost remains active for lead feel.
|===


The lead boost settings are close to rhythm but slightly altered for sustain and smoothness. The overdrive value remains very low because too much pedal drive makes fast leads smaller and more compressed.

The lead scene gets more mids and slightly more gain, but less low-end resonance than rhythm. This places the lead above the band without adding low-frequency clutter.

== Adaptive Gate (S/C)

[%autowidth,cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
|Parameter |Setting |Why this value is used
|Noise Reduction |76.0% |Lower than rhythm so bends, vibrato, legato, and harmonics sustain naturally.
|Source / Trigger |In 1 |The gate follows the clean DI transient.
|Bypass |Off |Still needed for high-gain noise, but less aggressive than rhythm.
|===


If sustain is still being cut off, lower Noise Reduction to `70%` to `72%`. Do this before adding more amp gain.

== Digital Delay (M)

[%autowidth,cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
|Parameter |Setting |Why this value is used
|Mix |12% |Slightly drier than the 6505 lead for better separation in harmonized lines.
|Feedback |26% |Controlled repeats.
|High Pass |260 Hz |Keeps delay out of the low mids.
|Low Pass |3900 Hz |Darker repeats, suited to the smoother lead role.
|Sync |Off |Fixed delay avoids identical timing with the other guitar.
|Sync Note |Ignored when Sync is Off |No tempo subdivision is active.
|Delay Time |390 ms |Shorter than the 6505 lead delay for separation.
|Mod Rate |0.25 Hz |Very slow movement.
|Mod Depth |7% |Minimal modulation for stable harmonized leads.
|Trails |On |Natural decay when leaving the lead scene.
|Bypass |Off |Delay is active for lead.
|===


Lead delay adds sustain and dimension. The repeats are high-passed and low-passed so they remain behind the dry lead and do not add fizz or low-mid mud.

== Plate

[%autowidth,cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
|Parameter |Setting |Why this value is used
|Mix |7% |Very low reverb to keep the smoother lead focused.
|Decay |44% |Short tail for fast metal leads.
|Pre Delay |20.0 ms |Keeps dry notes in front.
|High Pass |260 Hz |Keeps the reverb from filling low mids.
|Low Pass |3400 Hz |Darkens the tail.
|LF Damping |58% |Controls lower reverb buildup.
|HF Damping |72% |Smooth high-frequency tail.
|Mod Depth |6% |Minimal modulation.
|Trails |On |Natural decay after scene changes.
|Bypass |Off |Reverb is active but very subtle.
|===


Lead reverb is intentionally much lower than clean reverb. In this style, delay supplies most of the space, while a small plate prevents the lead from sounding pasted on top of the mix.

== Lead correction workflow

[%autowidth,cols="2,2,3",options="header"]
|===
|Problem |Change |Reason
|Lead sustain gets chopped |Lower Adaptive Gate to 70 to 72% |The gate is more likely than the amp to kill sustain.
|Lead is buried |Raise final Gain by +0.5 to +1.0 dB or add +1 dB in the primary lead-mid band |Volume and mids solve audibility better than extra gain.
|Lead is fizzy |Lower High Cut to 7.4 to 7.6 kHz or cut the 3.7 to 3.9 kHz band another -1 dB |This controls harshness while preserving note focus.
|Delay clouds fast runs |Lower Delay Mix to 8 to 10% and Feedback to 20 to 24% |Fewer repeats create more clarity.
|Lead feels too small |Raise mids before raising bass or resonance |Mids increase perceived size without fighting bass guitar.
|===
